The Process: Getting Started

March 30th, 2017

Hey there! So I see that you're interested in what the entire process was like huh? Well a little background story... I took an HTML&CSS course when I was in college so I had a general understanding of how things are done, as well as gained the skills to create the most basic, and dare I say sucky, looking websites.

Fast forward to 2016, during my second year of university I decided to take a leap of faith and pursue a major in computer science. I was scared. I constantly wondered if I was any good for it. I'm glad I made the decision and I love what I learn. It shouldn't matter if you;re scared of not being good at something, as long as you are willing to put in the work to get better then you will see the fruits of your labour.

Anyway, so how did I get started on building my own website? Well honestly it wouldn't have been made possible without my mentor, Kira (if you're reading this, Thank you!!). In 2017 I decided to take part of McGill's Women in Computer Science Industry Mentorship program and got paired up with a mentor who worked in a field of my interest (which is web development if you haven't already noticed). Wanting to take advantage of this opportunity, I decided that I should build my own website. It acts as a great resume too! I learned a lot of things. Buying a domain, hosting it, github (which is amazing!), and even finding out that you can make your own 404.html page.

Building my website and seeing it come to life day by day was something I always felt so proud of. Along the way, I learned more about HTML and CSS that I didn't know before, and started dabbling in a bit of Javascript. I also found a new little hobby! CSS animations, which you should totally check out here! What excites me the most is that I'm always learning something new. The best way to learn is to try things out and see how they work.

I hope you have enjoyed the website as much as I did creating it. Try testing it out on mobile devices! I tried to develop this as a responsive, mobile first website. If you see anything weird please let me know so I can fix it!

Sincerely,
Ha Mi Nguyen
